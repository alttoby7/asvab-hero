# Outreach Kit Revisions (2026-06)

Drop-in replacements for the Google Drive `outreach-kit-2026-05-20.md`, updated
now that the **embeddable AFQT widget** (`/embed`) shipped and
`/counselor-resources` was reworked to lead with our free tools.

**Two strategic changes:**
1. **Repoint the ask from the bare homepage to specific deep assets.** Resource
   curators add specific useful URLs, not a domain root. Lead with
   `/afqt-calculator` (the single best "try it now" link), plus `/calculator`
   and `/practice-test`.
2. **`/counselor-resources` is credibility collateral, not the ask.** It proves
   we are legit; it is not the thing we ask them to link. The link target is a
   free tool, or — strongest of all — the **embed**.

---

## 1. Linkable assets (replaces the "What we offer" asset list)

Use the right one per target:

- **Embeddable AFQT calculator** (`/embed`) — the strongest ask. Schools drop it
  into their page with one line of HTML; students use it without leaving their
  site. This is a far better pitch than "please link us" because it gives the
  school a working tool, and it carries a backlink automatically.
- **Free ASVAB calculators:** AFQT (`/afqt-calculator`), full all-branch
  (`/calculator`), GT score, line score, score converter.
- **Free ASVAB practice test** (`/practice-test`) and per-subtest drills.
- **Free study guides** (`/asvab-study-guide`), one per subtest topic.
- **2026 score-requirement references** by branch (`/asvab-score-requirements`).
- **Credibility collateral (not the ask):** the source-cited counselor reference
  at `/counselor-resources` — reference it to show you are not a link spammer.

## 2. Template A — Resource-page add (REVISED)

For counselors, JROTC, libraries, .edu vet centers. The change vs the old
Template A: link a specific deep asset, not `asvabhero.com`.

> **Subject:** Free ASVAB tool for your resources page
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I came across your [page name] page while looking for ASVAB resources for
> students, and it is a genuinely useful list.
>
> I help run ASVAB Hero, a free site for people preparing for the test. One
> piece that might fit your page is our free AFQT calculator: students enter
> their practice scores and instantly see their AFQT percentile and which
> branches and jobs they qualify for. It is free with no login required.
>
> Here is the link to add: https://asvabhero.com/afqt-calculator
>
> We also have a free practice test (https://asvabhero.com/practice-test) and a
> full all-branch calculator (https://asvabhero.com/calculator) if either is a
> better fit for your list. If you want to verify we are a real, independent
> resource, our counselor reference page cites every official source:
> https://asvabhero.com/counselor-resources
>
> Either way, thanks for keeping that page up to date. It clearly helps the
> students who find it.
>
> Best,
> [Your name]
> ASVAB Hero

*Personalize:* the page name, one specific detail you saw on their page, and the
single asset link that best matches what their page already covers (calculator
vs practice test vs study guides).

## 4. SOP changes (replaces the link-target guidance)

- **Link target priority:** (1) offer the **embed** (`/embed`) where the platform
  supports it — best yield; (2) else a specific **deep tool link**
  (`/afqt-calculator` first, then `/practice-test` or `/calculator`); (3) never
  pitch the bare homepage.
- **`/counselor-resources` is credibility only** — cite it to prove legitimacy,
  do not ask them to link it.
- **Best converters now:** LibGuide authors and Google-Sites-based JROTC/counselor
  pages (both embed-capable → use Template D). Branded/official sites still
  lowest-yield; pitch last.
- **Everything else in the Drive SOP stands:** ≤8–10 personalized emails/week,
  one follow-up, log every target. See `docs/outreach-deliverability.md` for the
  sending-hygiene rules (manual from Google Workspace, never a third-party tool
  on the root domain).
