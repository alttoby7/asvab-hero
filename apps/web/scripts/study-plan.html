Start here: only 4 subtests decide if you qualify

The ASVAB has 9 subtests. Only 4 of them feed your AFQT, the percentile that decides whether you get in at all:

  • AR Arithmetic Reasoning (math word problems)
  • WK Word Knowledge (vocabulary)
  • PC Paragraph Comprehension (short reading passages)
  • MK Mathematics Knowledge (algebra and geometry)

The formula is AFQT raw = 2(WK + PC) + AR + MK. Verbal counts twice. That means every point you raise on Word Knowledge or Paragraph Comprehension is worth double on your AFQT.

Spend 80% of week 1 on WK, PC, and AR. If you only have 30 minutes a day, do not study General Science, Auto & Shop, or Electronics in your first two weeks. They feed line scores for specific jobs, not the floor that decides if any branch will take you.

The 30-minute daily routine

This is the schedule that beats 90 minutes of unfocused review:

MinutesBlockWhat you actually do
0–5Vocab warmup10 Word Knowledge flashcards from yesterday's pile. Drop ones you got right twice.
5–15Subtest drill10 timed practice questions on today's subtest. Use a real timer. 11 seconds per WK, 36 seconds per AR.
15–22Review wrong answersFor every miss, write one sentence on why the right answer is right. Not the rule. The reason.
22–30New materialOne new prefix or root (WK day), one new formula (AR/MK day), or one new question type (PC day).
The 11-second rule. The CAT-ASVAB gives you about 11 seconds per Word Knowledge question. If you can't answer in 11 seconds at home, you won't on test day. Train under a timer from week 1. Do not let yourself ponder.

Weeks 3–4: Open the right doors

Once your AFQT clears the floor, the game changes. Now you're optimizing for line scores so the recruiter shows you the jobs you want, not the ones they need to fill.

Week 3 — Line scores you actually need

Find your target job. Look up its line-score formula. Drill the 1–2 subtests you're weakest in. Examples:

  • Army GT (most MOS doors): AR + WK + PC. Same as AFQT verbal — direct overlap.
  • Air Force G (intel, admin): WK + PC + sometimes MK
  • Navy ratings: highly varied. Run your scores through asvabhero.com/calculator first.
  • Marines GT: same as Army GT but the Corps weights it differently for promotions later.

If you already tested: the 1/1/6 rule

Every branch follows the same retake spacing:

AttemptWait time before retake
1st retake (after first test)1 calendar month
2nd retake1 more calendar month
3rd retake and beyond6 calendar months

One trap: if your score jumps 20+ points in 6 months, DoD requires a Confirmation Test (C-Test). If you score lower on the C-Test, the lower score replaces your retake. Crammers lose both scores this way.

Aim for a gain you can hold. A real +12 from a focused 30-day plan beats a fluky +25 you can't replicate. The C-Test is the same test, same conditions. If you earned the points, you keep them.
