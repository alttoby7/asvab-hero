Quick one this month. Two questions that trip people up, why they trip people up, and one high-ASVAB job worth aiming for. No cramming required.

Question 1 (Word Knowledge)

"Terse" most nearly means:
A) rude   B) concise   C) tense   D) lengthy

Most people pick C because "terse" sounds like "tense." That is the trap. The answer is B, concise. Terse means brief and to the point. When a word looks or sounds like another word, that lookalike is almost always the wrong answer they planted on purpose. Read all four options before you commit.

Question 2 (Arithmetic Reasoning)

A car travels 60 miles in 1.5 hours. At that same rate, how far will it travel in 4 hours?

The miss here is grabbing the numbers and guessing. Do it in two clean steps instead. First find the rate: 60 divided by 1.5 = 40 miles per hour. Then apply it: 40 x 4 = 160 miles. AR rewards writing the equation before you touch the arithmetic. Set it up, then solve it.

One takeaway

Both misses come from the same habit: reacting to the question instead of reading it. Slow down for one extra second, name what is actually being asked, and a surprising number of "hard" questions turn easy.
