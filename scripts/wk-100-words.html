How WK questions actually work

Every Word Knowledge question gives you a target word (often inside a short sentence with the word in bold) and four answer choices. The choices are almost always single-word synonyms.

The 11-second move: read the sentence, mentally cover the bold word, predict your own synonym from context, then match it to the closest answer. Don't read all four choices first — their job is to confuse you. Bring your answer to them.

5-question mini quiz (real WK format)

Read the sentence. Predict your own synonym before reading the choices. Then match.

1. The medic worked to mitigate the soldier's pain before evacuation.
  1. worsen
  2. reduce
  3. diagnose
  4. conceal
2. The squad leader gave ambiguous orders that left everyone confused.
  1. decisive
  2. shouted
  3. unclear
  4. brief
3. She was nonplussed by the unexpected question from the panel.
  1. relieved
  2. confused
  3. amused
  4. calm
4. The drill instructor told us to peruse the manual before drill.
  1. skim
  2. copy
  3. read carefully
  4. memorize quotes from
5. His tenacious grip on the rope kept the climber from falling.
  1. weak
  2. persistent
  3. sweaty
  4. casual
Answer key: 1) B reduce — 2) C unclear — 3) B confused (the classic trap is D “calm”) — 4) C read carefully (NOT A skim) — 5) B persistent.
